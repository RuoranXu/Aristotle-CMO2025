Let \( n \) be an odd positive integer, and satisfy: for any prime \( p \mid n \), \( \gcd(p-1, n) = 1 \). Find all \( (a, b, c) \in \mathbb{Z}_n^3 \) such that \( \gcd(a, b, c, n) = 1 \), and there exists a permutation \( (x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n) \) of \( 1, 2, \dots, n \) satisfying
\[
n \mid (a x_{i+2} + b x_{i+1} + c x_i) \tag{3}
\]
for all \( 1 \leq i \leq n \) (indices are modulo \( n \)).
